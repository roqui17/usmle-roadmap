USMLE + ECFMG Roadmap (International Medical Graduate)

Conservative, part-time plan starting March 2026. Use this as a printable handout.
Core idea: do not pick exam dates first. You earn exam dates with objective readiness (practice exams).

The WRONG way (common)

  • Pick Step 1 date early and rush to meet it.
  • Study passively (videos only, reading only) and delay question practice.
  • Ignore OET until the end because it “seems easy”.
  • Sit exams “to see how it goes”.

Result: higher failure risk, expensive retakes, and potentially losing an entire residency application cycle.

The RIGHT way (low risk)

  • Start prep, build foundation, and open ECFMG account early.
  • Use UWorld and timed practice early. Track performance trends.
  • Do OET early to remove administrative risk.
  • Schedule Step 1 only after consistent passing on NBME practice exams.

Result: fewer surprises, fewer delays, better Step 2 CK performance.

What she must complete (facts, no myths)

USMLE Step 1 USMLE Step 2 CK OET Medicine ECFMG Certification
  • ECFMG certification requires: Step 1 + Step 2 CK + OET Medicine (pass all 4 sections in one sitting). Source: https://www.ecfmg.org/certification/
  • IELTS is not the requirement for ECFMG. OET Medicine is. Source: https://www.ecfmg.org/certification-requirements-2024.html
  • Myth check: she does not need ECFMG certification to register for Step 1 or Step 2 CK. She needs an ECFMG account and credential verification. Source: https://www.ecfmg.org/registration-and-exam-scheduling/
  • USMLE exam overview: https://www.usmle.org/step-exams

Common failure points (where people misjudge)

OET Medicine (hidden risk)

  • Most common weak area: Writing (medical letter structure and tone).
  • Requirement: Grade B (350+) in each section, same sitting.

Official OET info: https://www.occupationalenglishtest.org/tests/medicine/

Step 1 (timing mistake)

  • Failure driver is usually readiness miscalibration, not “not enough resources”.
  • Don’t schedule based on emotion or social pressure.

USMLE performance data (includes pass rates by group): https://www.usmle.org/performance-data

Step 2 CK (career-defining)

  • For IMGs, this score is often the strongest numeric signal for competitiveness.
  • Rushing Step 2 CK can lock her out of programs and specialties.

NRMP Match data and IMG outcomes: https://www.nrmp.org/match-data-analytics/

English impact (realistic)

  • English mainly affects speed and fatigue on timed blocks.
  • Fix with timed question practice early, not only grammar work.

ECFMG English pathway overview: https://www.ecfmg.org/certification-requirements-2024.html

Readiness gates (how to decide dates the RIGHT way)

Rule: practice exams decide dates

  • Step 1: schedule only after multiple NBME practice exams show a comfortable passing margin and timed blocks feel sustainable.
  • Step 2 CK: schedule only after stable self-assessment performance and a complete UWorld pass.
  • OET: schedule early, and plan a retake buffer (writing is the usual reason for retakes).

USMLE exam prep hub: https://www.usmle.org/prepare-your-exam

Month-by-month guide (conservative, part-time, start March 2026)

Assumption: part-time means about 2 to 4 hours/day on weekdays, plus 4 to 6 hours/day on weekends when possible. Adjust up or down, but keep the structure.

Month Primary focus Deliverables and “do not skip”
Mar 2026 Setup + Step 1 foundation.
Start OET lightly in parallel.
  • Create ECFMG account (IWA) and start credential verification.
  • Begin Step 1 core review + begin UWorld (slow, tutor mode ok).
  • OET: baseline diagnostic, start writing templates.
ECFMG registration: https://www.ecfmg.org/registration-and-exam-scheduling/
Apr 2026 Step 1 systems-based learning + active recall.
  • UWorld Step 1: steady cadence, begin timed blocks weekly.
  • Anki or other spaced repetition for weak topics.
  • OET: 2 writing tasks/week with feedback.
May 2026 Step 1 question volume increases.
  • Increase timed blocks. Track error patterns (knowledge vs reading vs time).
  • OET: mock speaking role-plays, refine writing structure.
  • Start looking at OET test dates and seats.
Jun 2026 OET exam month (preferred) + Step 1 continues.
  • Sit OET Medicine (preferred window: June).
  • Step 1: keep minimum 4 to 5 timed blocks/week if possible.
  • Do not pause Step 1 completely for OET. Keep momentum.
OET Medicine info: https://www.occupationalenglishtest.org/tests/medicine/
Jul 2026 OET backup month + Step 1 consolidation.
  • If not taken in June, sit OET in July.
  • If OET result is below B in any section, book a retake promptly.
  • Step 1: finish major content areas and keep UWorld moving.
Aug 2026 Step 1 ramp-up.
  • First NBME self-assessment (baseline). Use it to plan, not to judge.
  • Switch more to timed, mixed blocks.
  • Identify top 5 weakest topics and build a remediation plan.
USMLE prep hub: https://www.usmle.org/prepare-your-exam
Sep 2026 Step 1 readiness building.
  • NBME every 4 to 6 weeks. Look for trend improvement.
  • Complete at least one full UWorld pass by end of this phase if possible.
  • Do not schedule Step 1 unless practice exams consistently pass.
Oct 2026 Step 1: move from learning to performance.
  • More mixed-question blocks; fewer new resources.
  • Second NBME. If not passing, continue building.
  • If passing comfortably, start considering a test window.
Nov 2026 Step 1: lock weak areas.
  • Targeted review only, plus high volume timed blocks.
  • Third NBME. If 2 to 3 consecutive passes, schedule Step 1.
Dec 2026 Conservative Step 1 test window.
  • Sit Step 1 only if readiness gates are met.
  • If not, push to Jan or Feb 2027 rather than force it.
USMLE Steps overview: https://www.usmle.org/step-exams
Jan 2027 Buffer month (Step 1 retake risk control or delayed Step 1).
  • If Step 1 not yet taken, this is a safer late window for part-time learners.
  • If Step 1 is done, begin Step 2 CK basics and a short reset.
Feb 2027 Step 2 CK start (foundation phase).
  • Start UWorld Step 2 CK (systems-based, then mixed).
  • Build daily routine: timed blocks + review.
Mar to May 2027 Step 2 CK ramp and scoring focus.
  • Complete UWorld Step 2 CK once. Review mistakes aggressively.
  • Take NBME or other official self-assessments for trend tracking.
  • Aim to schedule only once the score trend is stable.
Jun to Jul 2027 Conservative Step 2 CK test window.
  • Sit Step 2 CK when stable on self-assessments.
  • If OET was not passed earlier, fix it now (do not delay further).
Aug to Sep 2027 ECFMG certification application and processing.
  • Once Step 1 + Step 2 CK + OET are complete, apply for ECFMG certification.
  • Begin planning US clinical exposure and letters if residency is the goal.
ECFMG certification: https://www.ecfmg.org/certification/

If she can spend time in the US (how to use it strategically)

  • After Step 1, prioritize observerships or externships for US clinical exposure and letters of recommendation.
  • Volunteer or shadowing opportunities can help with interview readiness and communication confidence.
  • Networking with program-affiliated clinicians can materially improve residency chances.

Match outcomes and analytics: https://www.nrmp.org/match-data-analytics/

Quick checklist (printable)

  • Confirm medical school eligibility in the World Directory: https://search.wdoms.org/
  • Create ECFMG account and start credential verification: https://www.ecfmg.org/registration-and-exam-scheduling/
  • Begin Step 1 prep with early question practice (UWorld).
  • Begin OET prep early (especially Writing), sit by June or July 2026.
  • Schedule Step 1 only after consistent NBME passing trend.
  • Do Step 2 CK with scoring focus, do not rush it.
  • Apply for ECFMG certification after Step 1 + Step 2 CK + OET are complete.
